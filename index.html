An Introduction to
KLE Templates

Welcome! This is a guide to Keele Learning Environment (KLE) templates. It'll explain what they are, why they're useful and how you can add one to a KLE course. Don't panic, it's easier than you think!


If you're only looking to download a template, please click here.

What is a KLE template?

A template is a recommended layout, helping you to begin building your KLE course with a basic navigational structure. By adding a template, you will pre-populate the navigation menu with a set of standard folders that'll make it easier for students to find learning resources, assessment information etc.


It is strongly recommended that a template is applied before beginning to upload materials to the KLE.

Why were KLE templates introduced?

KLE templates have been developed in response to feedback from the Keele University IT survey and other evaluation activities, which highlighted some students having difficulty locating electronic resources. Templates are commonly used at other institutions and widely accepted as good practice.


They also help to provide a more consistent experience across modules and make it easier for staff to deliver content in a familiar style they feel comfortable with.

How do I know if I need a KLE template?

For most Keele modules, a template will need to be applied before uploading materials to the KLE, however, there are exceptions.


Please find below a list of frequently asked questions to help you decide whether you need a KLE template. If you're still not sure, please speak to your local learning technology support person.


My School or subject area already use their own template.
In some schools or subject areas, adding a template before teaching begins is already standard practice and is managed in different ways. If this applies to your module, it is recommended that you speak to the School Office or get in touch with your local learning technology support contact to find out more.
I have already built a well structured KLE course in a previous year/semester.
It is important to remember that these templates are designed as a starting point and do not intend to restrict innovation or replace existing good practice. If you have already spent a significant amount of time building a structure that works for you and your students then you may wish to use Blackboard's Course Copy feature instead.
I have a large amount of content to copy from a module I have taught previously.
Although it may be tempting to simply 'roll over' an entire module's content from one year to the next, it is often less time consuming to apply a fresh template and selectively copy individual folders or items. By doing so, it avoids outdated content (e.g. module handbooks, reading lists, assignment details etc.) appearing in your new KLE module and the risk of carrying over unwanted Grade Centre data which can hinder the assessment process.
A template was applied the last time I taught this module, so why does it need to be added again?
A new blank KLE occurance is created each time a module is taught. Although the previous instance will still be available, students studying the module for the first time will be automatically added to the latest version.

How should I use a KLE template?

Templates are only designed to set up a top-level, basic folder structure. However, once a template has been applied, being mindful of how and where content is uploaded will further improve your KLE course design.

For example, avoiding confusing folder structures and including helpful descriptions should always be a consideration when building an accessible KLE course.
